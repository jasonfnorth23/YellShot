WTF is this?

It's called YellShot — a platform that lets you send anonymous voice messages to someone's phone, delivered by an AI-powered character like Captain Cutdeep or Sister Sorry.

Think of it as outsourcing your awkward, savage, or ridiculous message to a fictional call center… so you don't have to say it yourself.

Breakups, roasts, confessions, birthday chaos — we make the call so you can stay mysterious.

Do I get proof the message was received?

Yes! If they answer or call back, we record the call — and their reaction is often hilarious. You'll get the audio by email. If they ghost entirely, we'll still confirm that the text version was delivered.

What if the call doesn't go through?

  • If they don't answer or hang up, we send a callback link via text (valid for 10 days)
  • We retry the call 1 hour later
  • If they still don't respond, your message is sent via text as a last resort

What is the success rate?

  • 99% of messages are delivered:
  • 50% pick up on the first call
  • 30% click the callback link
  • 19% get the message via text
  • Fewer than 1% fail due to rare technical issues

Are failures refunded?

No. To prevent abuse, we don't offer refunds for undelivered messages — otherwise trolls could game the system.
That said, with our 99% success rate, you're unlikely to need one.

How will the recipient know it's legit?

Each call starts with a surreal-but-official intro like:
"This is Captain Cutdeep from the Bureau of Brutal Honesty..."
We also send a follow-up text confirming it came from YellShot.com.

Can I stay anonymous?

Yes. You don't have to include your name unless you want to.
The persona delivers your message — they won't know it's you (unless you confess).

What if they cry?

Good. That means it worked.
(If they laugh instead, that's fine too.)
